New NCERT Class 9 Books (2026-27) Explained

👨‍🏫 By Shivam Sir 📅 Updated: 17 July 2026 ⏱ 6 min read
← All Articles

If your child has just entered Class 9, you may have noticed something surprising: every single NCERT textbook has changed. From the 2026-27 academic session, NCERT has replaced the old Class 9 books with completely new ones — new names, new chapters, and a new style of teaching. Books like Beehive, Moments and the old Mathematics textbook are gone.

As a teacher working with these new books every day in my live Class 9 batches, let me explain exactly what has changed, book by book, and what it means for your child's preparation.

Why Did NCERT Change the Class 9 Books?

The new books are part of the rollout of the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 and the National Curriculum Framework for School Education (NCF-SE) 2023. The goal is to move away from rote memorisation toward competency-based learning — understanding concepts, applying them to real life, and thinking critically.

You will notice this immediately in the new books: chapters open with real-life situations, activities and "think about it" questions appear throughout, and even the chapter names are more story-like. Mathematics and Social Science have been released as Part 1, with Part 2 arriving later in the session.

📐 Mathematics — Ganita Manjari (Part 1)

Ganita means mathematics and Manjari means a bouquet of flowers — NCERT presents each topic as a distinct "petal". Part 1 has 8 chapters:

  1. Orienting Yourself: The Use of Coordinates
  2. Introduction to Linear Polynomials
  3. The World of Numbers
  4. Exploring Algebraic Identities
  5. I'm Up and Down, and Round and Round (Circles)
  6. Measuring Space: Perimeter and Area
  7. The Mathematics of Maybe: Introduction to Probability
  8. Predicting What Comes Next: Exploring Sequences and Progressions

The big shifts: coordinate geometry now comes first, and topics like probability and sequences & progressions have entered Class 9. The book uses a "Low Threshold, High Ceiling" approach — every student can start easily, and stronger students can go deeper with starred (*) enrichment problems.

🔬 Science — Exploration

The new Science book, Exploration, integrates Physics, Chemistry, Biology and Earth Science into one activity-based volume with 13 chapters:

  1. Exploration: Entering the World of Secondary Science
  2. Cell: The Building Block of Life
  3. Tissues in Action
  4. Describing Motion Around Us
  5. Exploring Mixtures and their Separation
  6. How Forces Affect Motion
  7. Work, Energy, and Simple Machines
  8. Journey Inside the Atom
  9. Atomic Foundations of Matter
  10. Sound Waves: Characteristics and Applications
  11. Reproduction: How Life Continues
  12. Patterns in Life: Diversity and Classification
  13. Earth as a System: Energy, Matter, and Life

Every chapter is packed with hands-on activities, "Think It Over" questions, and "Pause and Ponder" self-checks after each section — the exam questions will increasingly test this application style.

🌍 Social Science — Understanding Society: India and Beyond (Part 1)

The biggest structural change: instead of four separate SST books, Class 9 now has one integrated book with all four disciplines inside. Part 1 has 9 chapters:

  • Introduction: 1. Understanding Social Science
  • Geography: 2. Shaping of the Earth's Surface · 3. Atmosphere and Climate
  • History: 4. Early Humans and Beginning of Civilisation · 5. State and Society up to 1000 CE
  • Political Science: 6. Democracy · 7. Elections
  • Economics: 8. Building Blocks in Economics: The Problem of Choice · 9. The Price Puzzle: What Drives the Market

📖 English — Kaveri & Hindi — Ganga

Kaveri replaces both Beehive and Moments with a single book of 8 prose chapters and 8 poems. The writing section is heavier now — persuasive essays, literary analysis, research writing and creative writing — while grammar remains familiar. For Hindi, the new book is Ganga, replacing the old Kshitij/Sparsh set.

⚠️ Important for parents: Old Class 9 guides, refreshers and last year's notes no longer match the syllabus. Chapters, order and question styles are all different. Class 10 books have NOT changed for 2026-27 — only Class 9 so far.

How Should Students Prepare with the New Books?

  1. Read the new NCERT line by line. With no old question banks matching, the textbook itself is now the single most important source — including the activities and "Think It Over" boxes.
  2. Make fresh chapter notes. Do not reuse a senior's notes; the content has genuinely changed. Our free study material page already lists every new chapter.
  3. Practise chapter-wise tests. Our free NCERT portal is updated with the new chapters — notes, homework and instant-scoring MCQ tests.
  4. Learn from a teacher who teaches the new books. Many tuitions are still teaching from old material. At Ramvas Gurukul, all Class 9 live batches run on Ganita Manjari, Exploration, Understanding Society, Kaveri and Ganga from day one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the old Class 9 books valid for 2026-27?

No. The chapters, structure and question styles have changed. Study only from the new NCERT textbooks.

Have the Class 10 books also changed?

No — Class 10 continues with the existing books for 2026-27. NCERT is changing the curriculum class by class, so the new Class 10 books are expected in a later session.

Where can I get free notes for the new books?

Right here — our study material page and NCERT notes & tests portal are fully updated for the new 2026-27 Class 9 books, free for every student.
